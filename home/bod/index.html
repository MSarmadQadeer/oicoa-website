The Board of Directors for the OICOA is composed of a President, Vice-President, Secretary General, and four other members, all of whom are Voting Members elected by the General Assembly.

The Secretary General of OICOA also serves as the ex-officio Secretary of the Board. The Board's powers and functions include approving the annual budget, authorizing expenditures, employing personnel, proposing amendments to the Statutes, concluding cooperation agreements, raising funds, and ensuring the effective application of the Statutes. They can also exercise powers of the General Assembly in emergencies, subject to ratification in the next session.

ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE BOARD OF DIRCETORS OF OICOA

1) Budgetary Oversight
Approve the annual budget and authorize expenditures on behalf of OICOA.
2) Operational Authorization
Empower the Secretary General/Executive Secretary to manage financial allocations, hire personnel, and disburse salaries.
3) Strategic Planning
Facilitate initiatives that align with and further the Association's objectives.
4) Statutory Amendments
Propose changes to the Statutes and seek approval for any modifications or repeals.
5) Fee Adjustments
Propose and secure approval for any changes in membership fees.
6) General Assembly Recommendations
Advise the General Assembly on matters specified in Statute 9(2).
7) Partnership Agreements
Establish cooperation agreements with analogous organizations to advance OICOA’s goals.
8) Fundraising
Spearhead efforts to secure funds for OICOA through various means, including donations, grants, loans, and membership fees.
9) Delegation of Powers
Assign specific powers to the Executive Committee, Special Committees, or other officers of OICOA as needed.
10) Policy Development
Ensure the Statutes are effectively applied and formulate rules, procedures, and policies for the Association's efficient operation.
11) Emergency Powers
In urgent situations, the Board can act on behalf of the General Assembly, with actions subject to subsequent ratification.
12) Proactive Decision-Making
In areas not explicitly reserved for the General Assembly or assigned to the Board, the Board can act as needed, with decisions subject to later approval by the General Assembly.
